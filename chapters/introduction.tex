\section{Motivation \& Problem Statement}

\hlnotein{Micro-UAVs}{I think we still need to put the blimp result in this thesis right? if so its important to also bring that up here} have expanded into applications such as indoor inspection, environmental monitoring, 
and inventory management, yet their capabilities remain constrained by short flight endurance and
restricted navigation in \hlnote{GPS-denied environments}{its good to mentioned the application here}.

Light-based navigation offers a \hlnote{cost-effective solution}{What is more than cost-effective? I wont add a small comparison here.}
for GPS-denied environments, as only a simple modulator is needed to turn existing lights into navigation 
beacons while the drone carries all sensing hardware.

Existing VLC-based localisation systems achieve high accuracy but require dense lighting infrastructure, 
and approaches that work with a single light source are typically limited to \hlnotein{bearing estimation,
which does not support spatial navigation around obstacles.}{its too detailed to bring up bearing estimation here,
just say it is limited to simple target tracking, and it does not provide abilitiy to avoid obstacles.}

Recent work has shown that a single light source can serve as a navigation beacon for micro-drones using a photodiode-based bearing estimation algorithm, but the resulting navigation remains reactive and does not exploit spatial information about the light environment.

This thesis addresses this limitation by introducing a gradient-ascent controller that fuses bearing information with a spatial light intensity map, enabling more robust and tunable autonomous navigation toward a light source.
