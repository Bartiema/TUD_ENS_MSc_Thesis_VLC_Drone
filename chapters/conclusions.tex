\chapter{Conclusions}
\label{chp:conclusions}

\section{Summary}

This thesis presented a fused gradient-ascent navigation controller that enables a micro-drone to 
navigate toward a light source using a combination of bearing angle estimation 
and a self-built light intensity map.

The controller was validated in simulation, demonstrating obstacle avoidance capability 
that the bearing-only baseline cannot achieve, and then successfully ported to a \hlnote{CrazyFlie~2.1}{I think 
we should also put mine blimp experiment in real world, it will link more to the simulation.} 
equipped with a custom miniaturised photodiode PCB.

Real-world experiments confirmed that the SNR-based signal processing pipeline 
effectively isolates the target beacon from ambient light, and that the fused controller 
navigates correctly in both open-space and single-obstacle scenarios.
